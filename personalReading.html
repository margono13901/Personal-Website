My Reading List

These are some books that have influenced me

The Art Of War

Sun Tzu

Sun Tzu wrote this classic book of military strategy based on Chinese warfare and military thought. I find this book goes beyond its inital purpose, as it can serve as a guide to resolve conflicts in relationships, business, and in life.

The Black Swan

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Taleb shows in a playful way that Black Swan events explain almost everything about our world, and yet the experts are blind to them. I find that anyone who reads this book will realize that our entire society has a load of epistemological problems that cause disasters when we try to use statistics to solve social issues.

Like A Virgin

Richard Branson

Richard Branson offers his views on what makes a good business and leader. Its refreshing to see a qualitative approach based on business culture, customer satisfaction, and relationships when most business teachings rely on more quantitative methods on determining the value of a business.

The Age of Cryptocurrency: How Bitcoin and Digital Money Are Challanging The Global Economic Order

Pual Vigna and Michael J Casey

This book explores the history of cryptocurrencies, what it is, and how it can transform our world.

The Essential Drucker: The Best of Sixty Years of Peter Drucker's Essential Writings on Management

Peter F. Drucker

Father of modern management, social commentator, and preeminent business philosopher, Peter F. Drucker analyzed economics and society for more than sixty years. I think that anyone who reads this book can get a great feel for management, and see how his teachings influence modern practices.

Give and Take: A Revolutionary Approach to Success

Adam Grant

Today's world is dependent on relationships to furthur business goals. Grant offers an interesting account that conflicts what most people think is the route to success. By being a non-pushover giver, you can accel past your taker peers.

Hooked: A Revolutionary Approach to Success

Nir Eyal

A great book to help you understand why you are so addicted to Facebook and help you build something as engaging. Theres a whole section on the ethics of this method since the method is so powerful.

The Intellegent Investor

Benjamin Graham

Warren Buffet's bible and an essential to anyone who wants to start investing.

King of Capital: The Remarkable Rise, Fall, and Rise Again of Steve Schwarzman and Blackstone

David Carey and John E Morris

The story of Steve Schwarzman and Blackstone was the inspiration for me to get into finance. This story shows the rise of an entrepreneur, who went two years with barely any business into one of the powerhouses on wallstreet.

The Lean Start Up

Eric Ries

This book dispells almost all of your notions on how a startup works and how it succeeds. It's essential to understand product development and iteration.

The Personal MBA: Master The Art Of Business

Eric Ries

Save yourselfs thousands of dollars and just buy this book to understand the essentials of business.

SPIN Selling

Neil Rackham

An amazing approach on how to sell a product or idea. The author shows a clean and simple approach that dispells the art of selling and how it varies between cultures.

How To Win Friends and Influence People

Dale Carnegie

I feel that this is the most important book to understand human interaction out there. Heavily influenced my life and I would be a totally different person without it.

On Writing Well

William Zinsser

By reading this, you'll get an understanding that good writing and what you think is good writing are totally different.
